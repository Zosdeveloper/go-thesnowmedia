1. Funnel Architecture

Which Funnel Type for High-Ticket Services ($2,500+/mo)?

Answer: Application Funnel. Brunson's rule: the higher the price, the more qualification required before a conversation happens.

Application Funnel Structure (Page by Page)

  1. Landing/Opt-in Page (the "gate") - Headline focused on transformation, short VSL or compelling copy, social proof, CTA: "Apply Now" or "See If You Qualify". No price mentioned.
  2. Application Form - 5-10 qualifying questions designed to disqualify bad leads and make good leads self-identify. The act of filling it out increases commitment.
  3. Thank You / Calendar Page - "Your application is being reviewed" (even if auto-approved). Calendar embed. Short video setting expectations. Reinforce urgency.

Traffic Temperature Matching

TemperatureAwarenessFunnel Match
ColdProblem-aware, don't know solutions or youBridge page first, then funnel
WarmSolution-aware, evaluating optionsGo direct to landing page
HotBrand-aware, trust youGo direct to offer/application

Google Ads = warm traffic. Skip bridge pages. Go direct to the application landing page.

2. Copywriting Frameworks

Hook, Story, Offer (Master Structure)

  • Hook = headline + subhead. Stop the scroll in 3 seconds. Formula: "How to [result] without [painful method]"
  • Story = the Epiphany Bridge. Creates emotional buy-in before the logical offer. Transfers belief, doesn't explain features.
  • Offer = CTA block. Revealed only after the story has done its work.

Epiphany Bridge (8 Components)

  1. Backstory - where you were before (must be relatable)
  2. Desire - the specific result you wanted
  3. Wall - what blocked you (mirrors prospect's current situation)
  4. Epiphany - the single moment of realization
  5. Plan - the steps you took after that epiphany
  6. Conflict - what almost derailed you
  7. Achievement - the result
  8. Transformation - who you became (future pacing)

Short form for landing pages: Backstory + Wall + Epiphany + Transformation (3-4 paragraphs)

The Big Domino

ONE core belief. If the prospect believes this, all other objections dissolve.

Formula: "If I can make them believe [new opportunity] is the key to [desired result] and is only attainable through [your method], then all other objections become irrelevant."

  • Home Services: "AI call handling is now table stakes for contractors competing on leads"
  • Consulting: "The bottleneck is not your marketing, it's the 60% of inquiries that never get a meaningful follow-up"

Headline Formulas

Curiosity-based (preferred for ad traffic): "How to [desired result] without [thing they hate/fear]"

Benefit-based (for warm/retargeting): States the outcome directly.

Brunson's rule: curiosity beats benefit on cold/warm traffic because people will not click on a benefit they don't believe yet.

Three Objection Types

  1. Vehicle: "This method (AI) doesn't work for my business"
  2. Internal: "Maybe it works for others but I couldn't make it work"
  3. External: "Even if I wanted to, I don't have time/budget"

Each story section on the page should neutralize one of these three. Don't list and answer directly (that signals weakness). Tell a story that makes the old belief look obviously wrong.

Power Words

"New opportunity" (never "improvement"), "secret", "without", "finally", "discovered", "imagine", "here's what I found..."

3. Visual Design Principles

What Brunson's Pages ACTUALLY Look Like (from screenshots)

Key Discovery: The research described dark pages. The actual screenshots show: dark hero section ONLY, then WHITE background for 80%+ of the page. Heavy use of real photography, long-form editorial copy, bold serif-style headlines.

Real Design Pattern (from Inner Circle + 2CCX)

ElementActual Style
HeroDark background (near-black), gold accents, auto-playing VSL
Below heroWHITE background, black text, 80%+ of page
HeadlinesMontserrat weight 800, large, bold. Sometimes serif for quotes.
Body fontOpen Sans, regular weight
CTA buttonsRed/maroon (rgb 154,50,61) or dark green (rgb 15,87,69), 12px radius
Gold accentUsed for labels, decorative elements, NOT for buttons
PhotosEverywhere. Real headshots, group photos, event photos, casual shots.

NavigationNone on sales pages. Minimal top bar with login on application pages.

4. VSL Script Structure (90 seconds to 3 minutes)

HOOK (0:00-0:15)
"If you're a [niche] and you're [painful situation],
this is the most important video you'll watch today."

PROBLEM (0:15-0:35)
"The reason most [niche] struggle with [outcome] isn't [obvious reason].
It's [real reason they haven't heard before]."

CREDIBILITY (0:35-0:55)
"My name is Milos. I've worked with [number] [niche] businesses
and helped them [specific result] using [named system]."

SOLUTION (0:55-1:30)
"I built something called [Name]. It [does X] so you can [outcome]
without [biggest objection]."

PROOF (1:30-1:50)
"[Client type] went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe].
[Second brief example]."

CTA (1:50-2:15)
"If you want the same, click the button below.
Fill out a quick form. I'll review your situation and
tell you exactly what I'd do first."

Production: Talking head, clean audio (non-negotiable), ring light. Authentic beats polished. Captions burned in for mobile. Embed via Vimeo/Wistia, not YouTube.

5. Post-Conversion Flow

Thank-You Page

Confirm action, deliver immediate value (60-sec prep video), micro-commitment (watch case study, join group).

Soap Opera Sequence (5 emails, 6 days)

DayEmailHook
0Confirmation"Here's exactly what we'll cover on your call."
1Backstory"I started Snow Media after watching agencies deliver dashboards nobody reads."
2Epiphany"The moment I realized AI could do in 60 seconds what takes a VA 6 hours."
3Case studyOne real client result with specifics.
4Hidden benefit"The real value isn't more leads. It's never losing the ones you already paid for."
5Urgency"Your call is tomorrow. Here's what to have ready."

Exit-Intent Popup

Alternate lower-commitment offer. "Not ready? Get the free breakdown: 5 Revenue Leaks Costing Contractors $10K+/Month." Single email field. Feeds into Seinfeld nurture sequence.

Retargeting

Different creative than landing page. Testimonial/case study angle. Never retarget to the same page with the same hook.

6. B2B Adaptations (What to Skip from Brunson)

Elements to SKIP for B2B services:
Brunson ElementWhy Skip It
The Stack (on landing page)Works on discovery calls. Kills credibility on B2B landing pages.
Countdown timersInstant trust killer with contractors and consultants.
Webinar funnelContractors won't sit through 90 min. Google Ads traffic too warm to gate.
Price on page$2,500+/mo discussed on call only.
Low-ticket tripwireNo $47 front-end product. Free strategy call IS the entry point.
"As Seen On" logosDon't have them. Don't fake them.
Dream outcome framingLead with cost of inaction, not "imagine your dream business."
Aggressive scarcity"Only 2 spots LEFT!!!" reads desperate. "We take on 3 partners/month" reads selective.
Over-hyped copyContractors are prevention-focused. "$8K/month lost" beats "imagine your dream."
Lifestyle AC archetypeAttractive Character must be credible operator, not lifestyle marketer.

What Works Perfectly for B2B (No Modification)

  • Application funnel before sales call
  • Inbound-first phone funnel (conversions 4-5x higher when prospects call you)
  • Specificity of avatar (contractor-specific language)
  • Value ladder as trust-building arc

Reference: Sabri Suby's HALO Model (Brunson adapted for B2B)

HVCO (High Value Content Offer) → VSL → Application → Call. Grew King Kong to $10M+ using this modified Brunson approach for B2B/DTC services.
