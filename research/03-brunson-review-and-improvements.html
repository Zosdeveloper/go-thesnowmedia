Brunson Review and Improvement Plan

Snow Media | April 16, 2026 | "Act as Russell Brunson" review + Design audit + Visual analysis

Part 1: Russell Brunson Review of V1 Pages

Pages reviewed as if Brunson himself was critiquing them. 10 issues identified, prioritized.

Issue 1: No Story (CRITICAL)

Problem: Zero Epiphany Bridge on either page. The Big Domino section is a statement, not a story. "You just STATED the belief. You didn't make them feel it."

Fix applied: Added 3-4 paragraph origin story between problem agitation and solution reveal. Different story per niche. Backstory, wall, epiphany, transformation structure.

Issue 2: Headline Is Benefit-First, Should Be Curiosity-First

Before: "Your Phones Ring After Hours. Nobody Answers. That Revenue Disappears Forever."

After: "How Contractors Are Recovering $15K+ Per Month in Lost Revenue Without Hiring a Single Person"

The "how...without" formula creates a curiosity gap. They HAVE to scroll to find out.

Issue 3: No Attractive Character

Problem: No name, no photo, no "I" on either page. Could be from any agency.

Fix applied: Added Milos's name, title, avatar placeholder. First-person voice throughout ("I built..." not "We built...").

Issue 4: Solution Reveal Is Features, Not Secrets

Before (Feature)After (Named Mechanism)
24/7 AI Call AnsweringThe After-Hours Revenue Recovery System
Automated Estimate Follow-UpThe Silent Closer
Lead Recovery EngineThe Dormant Revenue Reactivator
Real-Time DashboardThe Growth Scoreboard

Named mechanisms signal proprietary systems. Features are commodities.

Issue 5: Social Proof in Wrong Position

Before: Problem → Belief → Solution → CTA → Social Proof

After: Problem → Belief → Social Proof → Solution → CTA

You earn the right to present the solution by proving you've done it for others first.

Issue 6: Subheadline Gives Away the Answer

Before: "Discover the system that captures, follows up, and books every lead automatically."

After: "Discover the system 107 service businesses use to stop losing jobs to voicemail. Setup takes 30 minutes."

Tell them the RESULT and EASE. Hide the HOW. The "how" keeps them on the page.

Issue 7: No Before/After Transformation

Fix applied: Added two-column Before/After section with specific line items showing the contrast.

Issue 8: CTA Text Could Be Stronger

Before: "Apply for Your Free Strategy Call" (implies you might say no)

After: "See If You Qualify" (softer, curiosity-driven)

Issue 9: FAQ Title Is Generic

Before: "Common Questions"

After: "Questions We Hear Before Every First Call"

Issue 10: No Exit-Intent Mechanism

Fix applied: Created ExitIntentPopup component. Fires on mouse leave (5-second delay, once per session). Alternate offer: free guide with email capture.

Part 2: What Brunson Pages ACTUALLY Look Like

Critical Finding: Our V1 pages used an all-dark zinc-950 background. Brunson's actual pages use a dark hero ONLY, then WHITE background for 80%+ of the page. We were in "two different worlds."

What We Built vs What Brunson Actually Does

What We BuiltWhat Brunson Does
All-dark zinc-950 backgroundDark hero, then WHITE background for 80% of page
No imagery at allPhotos on nearly every scroll. Real people, real events.
Minimal "developer" aestheticLong-form, content-rich, editorial feel
Clean modern cardsSimple white sections with big text, inline images
Subtle muted tonesBold colors: dark red/maroon CTAs, green accent, gold highlights
Short punchy sectionsLong-form storytelling with multiple paragraphs
Gold/amber CTA buttonsRed/maroon buttons (rgb 154,50,61) or green (rgb 15,87,69)
Abstract mechanism names in cardsPersonal, conversational copy that reads like a letter

Brunson's Actual Design System (from Inner Circle + 2CCX screenshots)

  • Hero: Dark background (near-black), gold accents, VSL video center stage
  • Below hero: White background, black text. This is where 80% of content lives.
  • Headlines: Montserrat 800 weight, large, dark. Sometimes serif for pull-quotes.
  • Body: Open Sans, regular weight, dark gray/black text on white
  • CTA Buttons: Red/maroon (primary) or dark green (first CTA), NOT gold. Gold is accent only.
  • Photos: Real headshots, group photos, event photos. Inline with text, not in cards.
  • Video testimonials: Grid layout (video left, name below), multiple per section
  • Stats bar: Big numbers with labels (e.g., "401 Two Comma Club Winners")
  • Copy: Long-form personal letter style. Paragraphs, bold inline text, first-person
  • Scarcity: "ONLY 6 SPOTS LEFT" in bright text above CTA. Structural, not timer-based.

Part 3: Redesign Requirements

The redesign keeps ALL Brunson structural elements (copy, section order, funnel flow) but completely overhauls the visual layer to match his actual pages.

Visual Changes Needed

  1. Dark hero section ONLY. Everything below switches to white/light background.
  2. CTA buttons change from gold to red/maroon (primary) with white text.
  3. Gold accent stays for labels, decorative elements, pre-headlines only.
  4. Real photography: Milos headshot (mandatory), system mockup/dashboard visual, placeholder images for future event/client photos.
  5. Body copy on white background with dark text. Long-form editorial feel.
  6. Social proof cards: white cards with subtle shadow on light background, not dark cards.
  7. FAQ: clean on white background, not dark.
  8. Stats bar with big numbers, visible on light background.
  9. Montserrat stays for headlines. Add Open Sans for body text.

What Stays the Same

  • All copy (headlines, Epiphany Bridge, Big Domino, FAQ, CTAs)
  • Section order (Hero, Problem, Story, Big Domino, Social Proof, Solution, Before/After, CTA, FAQ, Final CTA)
  • Named mechanisms (After-Hours Revenue Recovery System, Silent Closer, etc.)
  • Exit-intent popup
  • Application form and thank-you page
  • No navigation, no footer links
  • Structural scarcity text
